About Me

My name is Naomi Conrad, and as of Summer 2026, I am an incoming senior at Gonzaga University purusing a B.S. in Civil engineering with concentrations in construction management and structural, a B.B.A. in accounting, and minors in Spanish and Business for Engineering Technologies. I am originally from Denver, Colorado and love being outdoors, skiing, biking, hiking, and running. This summer, I participated in a study abroad program in Delft in the Netherlands to study civil engineering infrastructure and sustainability, including traffic and water management infrastructure, as well as material sustainability. Studying abroad in the Netherlands has been an extraordinary opportunity and has creating a lasting impact on understanding the role of infrastructure on community culture and sustainability. After studying abroad, I chose to continue traveling around Europe through Belgium, Luxembourg, Germany, Austria, and, as of June 15, I am working on this website from Naples, Italy.

While I began my time at Gonzaga as a civil engineering major with an interest in structural engineering, through a construction concentration, I took introductory accounting courses for the first time my junior year. Though very late (relatively) in my college career, this sparked a sincere interest in accounting, leading me to pursue it as a major in addition to civil engineering, hence my place in this course. I understand this is an atypical route to take, and the truth is that I am not enitely sure which career path I will pursue post-grad, but having both degrees gives me the opportunity to pursue either.
